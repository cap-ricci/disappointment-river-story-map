Disappointment River

In 1789, the scottish explorer and trader Alexander Mackenzie set out on a long journey along the Deh Cho, the greatest river in Canada, in search of the Northwest Passage.

His solid faith on the success of the expedition was built upon the explorations of great men before him: Captain James Cook, explorer and fellow trader Peter Pond, and countless native americans whose name is lost. But at the end of the river he found only ice blocking the way, and not the Pacific ocean.

This is the story of this unsuccessful expedition, and of those that preceeded him, in light of "what if" Alexander could have paddled the river in our century.
